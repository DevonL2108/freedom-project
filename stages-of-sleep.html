Stages of Sleep

There are four stages of sleep:


  • Non-REM (NREM) sleep
    • Stage 1 (the lightest stage of NREM): the drowsy sleep stage that can be easily disrupted causing awakenings or arousals. The muscles within your body begin to relax and your brain activity begins to decrease.

    • Stage 2 (the first actual stage that defines NREM sleep): awakenings or arousals do not occur as easily as in stage 1 and slow moving eye rolls discontinue. Brain waves continue to slow with specific bursts of rapid activity known as sleep spindles intermixed with sleep structures known as K complexes. (they both serve as protection for the brain from awakening from sleep). Your body temperature begins to decrease and heart rate begins to slow.

    • Stage 3 (the deep stage of NREM sleep): the most restorative stage of sleep which consists of delta waves or slow waves. Awakenings and arousals are rare and often it is difficult to awaken someone in stage 3 sleep. Parasomnias (sleepwalking, sleep talking, and nightmares) occur during the deepest stage of sleep.

  • REM sleep (rapid eye movement)
    • Stage 4 (known as the dreaming stage): eye movement are rapid, moving from side to side and brain waves are more active than in stages 2 & 3 of sleep. awakening s and arousals can occur more easily in REM.
